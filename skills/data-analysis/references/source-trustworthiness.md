# Source Trustworthiness

Use this guidance whenever source quality materially affects the analysis.

## Source tiers

Prefer sources in this order:

1. Primary data or primary documents
2. Official documentation, filings, or first-party reports
3. Reputable secondary analysis
4. Expert commentary
5. Unverified summaries, reposts, or anonymous claims

## Evaluate each important source on

- origin: who produced it
- recency: when it was produced
- scope: what it actually covers
- methodology: how the information was produced
- independence: whether it has a strong incentive to distort
- consistency: whether it aligns with other credible sources

## Claim strength labels

Use these labels implicitly in your wording:

- strong: supported directly by high-quality evidence
- moderate: supported but with meaningful caveats
- weak: plausible but limited or indirect support

## Reporting rule

When evidence is weak or mixed, narrow the wording. For example:

- `the sources consistently show` for strong evidence
- `the available materials suggest` for moderate evidence
- `one possible interpretation is` for weak evidence
